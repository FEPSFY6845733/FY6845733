Abstract

As the world maximises potential in development, fossil fuels, the highest energy supplier used, are coming to an end. With the population constantly increasing, fossil fuels are being consumed at exponentially higher rates, causing the destruction of our climate and accelerating the use of leftover non-renewable sources. Looking for efficient alternatives is a struggle, with majority of all current outputs currently standing below 50% efficiency, including coal, natural gas, oil and even renewable sources such as solar panels. Piezoelectricity may be the future of energy supply, not only being renewable, but by also being over 95% efficient. Using mechanical stress, piezoelectric materials allow us to utilize wasted energy, supporting the cut of fossil fuels and replacing them with previously wasted energy. Although results of smaller scale examples may not seem like much, when larger quantities, piezoelectricity can be a strong solution in the future. With high use of vehicles, and therefore mechanical stress, built up areas with high traffic flow and roads under constant pressure, it can generate high amounts of energy.

What is Piezoelectricity?

In an ever-changing society that requires more focus on climate change, we urgently need to find alternative power sources. Sources that produce less carbon and greenhouse gases and are kinder to our environment, but are powerful enough to sustain the world we live in. One such solution is the use of piezoelectricity. Piezoelectric is the charge that is generated by certain solid materials, such as crystals and ceramics, which when placed under mechanical stress, can convert pressure and vibrations into electrical energy. An efficient way of utilizing these crystals would be through placement in highly used roads, with high amounts of traffic flow. The crystals will be placed around 5 centimetres below the asphalt or road surface and the pressure exerted on the roads from the vehicles will cause a deformation in the crystal. The deformation of the crystal allows wasted energy to be transferred into electrical energy. (KHplant) claims that the use of these renewable energy devices, if planted along one kilometre stretch of road, could provide an average of 400kW of power, enough to power 162 homes. Theoretically, the system can produce one kilowatt hour (kWh) of electricity for every 12 metres of road. To achieve this rate, the system must be implemented on fast, busy roads.

How Useful Is It?

The UK contributes less than 1% of all carbon emissions, therefore piezoelectric roads would be most successful in more highly populated areas like America and China, who contribute 14% and 30% of all global carbon emissions. That being said, major cities in the UK could possibly still see the benefits, but on smaller scale. The reason being is that built-up cities with large road networks would be ideal for this kind of infrastructure, as they are highly populated with plenty of traffic flow to make the crystals successful. If we were to total the area of public roads across just a few major cities in America, for example, New York City, Los Angeles, Chicago and Houston, this would mean a total of around 53,000 kilometres of road. Using the theoretical research collected, (assuming that there is an equal distribution of 10–16 mm diameter crystals) just these few major American cities could generate over 42.1 million watts of power annually with the use of piezoelectric crystals.

Furthermore, based on statistics from the year (2022), ‘Electricity consumption in the United States totalled 4,050 terawatt-hours, the highest value in the period under consideration’. This means that for the whole of America, the total annual value for power is in the region of 463 GW. Currently, 80% of all American energy production comes from fossil fuels which in turn, results in a staggering 370 billion watts being generated by the source. This indicates that although piezoelectricity doesn’t have a massive effect on the overall total annual power output, the crystals are still one of the more efficient alternates to fossil fuels, with an approximate efficiency of (95%) Fossil fuels only have an efficacy of approximately 35% and solar panels have 20%, indicating that a lot of the energy from the most frequently used sources is wasted. Not only are the piezoelectric crystals an efficient renewable energy source, but unlike other renewable energy sources, the crystals do not take up any extra land as they are placed 5cm under the asphalt.
